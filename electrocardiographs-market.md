<p><ul><li>Resting System</li><li>Stress System</li><li>Holter Monitor System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electrocardiographs are medical devices used to monitor and record the electrical activity of the heart. There are three main types of electrocardiograph systems on the market: resting systems, stress systems, and Holter monitor systems. Resting systems are used to record a patient's heart activity while at rest. Stress systems are designed to monitor heart activity during physical activity or stress tests. Holter monitor systems are portable devices worn by patients to continuously record heart activity over a 24-hour period. Each type of system serves a different purpose in diagnosing and monitoring heart conditions.</p></p>
