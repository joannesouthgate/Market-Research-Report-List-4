<p><p>Mobile network backhaul links facilitate the transfer of data between cell towers and the core network. They can be established using various technologies such as copper, fiber, microwave, and others. Copper backhaul links utilize existing infrastructure but have limited bandwidth. Fiber backhaul links offer high bandwidth and low latency but require extensive deployment. Microwave backhaul links provide a wireless alternative with quick deployment but limited capacity. Other technologies such as satellite and millimeter-wave are also used for backhaul links in certain situations.</p></p>
