<p><p>The Japanese sake market features various types based on rice polishing and brewing techniques. Ordinary sake is the most basic form. Junmai Ginjo, crafted from rice polished to at least 60%, delivers a refined taste. Similarly, Ginjo sake, also with a polishing ratio of 60%, emphasizes fruity and floral notes, appealing to connoisseurs. Each type caters to diverse preferences within the market.</p></p>
