<p><strong>The Frozen Skin Graft Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Split-thickness</li><li>Full-thickness</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Skin grafting is a common procedure used to treat severe burns, wounds, and skin injuries. In the market, there are two main types of skin grafts available: split-thickness and full-thickness. </p><p>Split-thickness skin grafts involve taking a thin layer of skin from a donor site, typically the thigh or buttocks, and placing it over the damaged area. This type of graft is often used for larger wounds or burns that require faster healing.</p><p>Full-thickness skin grafts, on the other hand, involve taking a thicker layer of skin, including the epidermis and dermis, from the donor site. This type of graft is usually used for smaller wounds or burns on highly visible areas, such as the face or hands, as it provides a more natural appearance and better long-term results.</p><p>Overall, both split-thickness and full-thickness skin grafts play important roles in the frozen skin graft market, catering to different patient needs and treatment requirements.</p></p>
