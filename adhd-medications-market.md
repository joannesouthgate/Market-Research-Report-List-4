<p><p>ADHD medications can be classified into two main types: stimulant and non-stimulant. Stimulant medications work by increasing the levels of certain neurotransmitters in the brain, which helps improve focus and attention. They are the most commonly prescribed medications for ADHD. On the other hand, non-stimulant medications work by targeting different neurotransmitters and can be an alternative for those who experience side effects from stimulants. Both types of medications aim to help manage symptoms of ADHD and improve overall functioning.</p></p>
