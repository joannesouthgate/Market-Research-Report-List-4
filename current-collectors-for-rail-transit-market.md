<p><p>Current collectors for rail transit can be classified into three main types based on their positioning: Top-Running, Bottom-Running, and Side-Running. Top-Running collectors are mounted on the top of the rail vehicle to draw power from an overhead wire. Bottom-Running collectors are located beneath the rail vehicle and collect power from a bottom contact rail. Side-Running collectors are positioned on the side of the vehicle and gather power from a side contact rail. Each type has its advantages and is used in different rail transit systems based on specific requirements.</p></p>
