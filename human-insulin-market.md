Insulin Analogs are synthetic versions of human insulin with altered molecular structures for faster or prolonged action. Biosimilars are highly similar versions of already-approved insulin products. Human Insulin Biologics are insulin products derived from living organisms using biotechnological processes. <p><p>The human insulin market is segmented into short-acting, intermediate-acting, long-acting, and pre-mix insulin based on their application. Long-acting insulin provides a steady release of insulin throughout the day. Pre-mix insulin combines short and intermediate-acting insulin for more convenient dosing. These different types of insulin cater to the varying needs of diabetic patients for effective blood sugar management.</p></p>
